        JoJo's Bizarre Adventure
        Roleplaying Game

        A 2d20 system adaptation for Parts 1–9. Rules only — no setting fluff.

        What You Need

        • 2–5 players and one Gamemaster (GM)
        • At least two d20 dice per player; at least four d6 (Combat Dice)
        • Tokens or paper for Action Points
        • Printable character sheet (3 pages) — Stand sheet — Interactive Character Sheet (editable online, trait & equipment pickers, inline rules, dice roller) — GM Panel (notepad, combat map, NPCs, Discord player view)
        • Pencil and paper for notes

        Core Rules

        Skill Tests

        When success is uncertain, roll a pool of 2–5 twenty-sided dice (d20). Each die that rolls equal to or under your target number scores one success. Meet or beat the difficulty to pass.

        1. Choose attribute + skill: GM sets combination (e.g. STR + Melee Weapons). Target number = attribute + skill rank.
        2. Set difficulty: Normally 1–5 successes required.
        3. Roll dice pool: Start with 2d20; buy up to 3 more with Action Points before rolling.
        4. Count successes: Each d20 ≤ TN = 1 success. Natural 1 = critical success (2 successes). Tag skills: any die ≤ skill rank = critical success.
        5. Compare to difficulty: Pass if successes ≥ difficulty. Extra successes become Action Points.
        6. Complications: Each natural 20 causes a complication after resolution (or GM gains 1 AP).

        Test Difficulty

        DifficultyExample
        0Routine; auto-success unless you choose to roll
        1Close-range shot, simple lock
        2Reinforced door, treat injury
        3Identify poison, disarm trap
        4Complex hack, landmine
        5Convince enemy to stand down, long-range storm shot

        Opposed Tests

        One side rolls first; their successes become the other's difficulty. In combat opposed tests, the defender spends AP first if buying dice. Environmental modifiers add to the winner's success total instead of difficulty.

        Assistance

        Roll 1d20 (no AP buys); add successes to ally's pool if they scored at least 1 success. Group tests: one leader, others assist.

        Action Points

        • Generated by extra successes on tests; group pool max 6 AP
        • Buy d20s: 1st die = 1 AP, 2nd = 2 AP, 3rd = 3 AP (max 5d20 total per test)
        • After test: Obtain information (1 AP), reduce time (1 AP), improve quality (1 AP), extra minor action (1 AP), extra major action (2 AP, +1 difficulty)
        • If no AP to buy dice, each AP you need gives the GM 1 AP (buy dice only)
        • GM starts each quest with 1 AP per player; no maximum

        Luck

        Luck points = Luck attribute at start of each quest. Refresh at milestones or new quest.

        SpendEffect
        Luck of the DrawIntroduce a helpful detail (GM may veto or require extra Luck)
        Stacked DeckBefore a test, use LCK instead of default attribute for TN
        Lucky TimingIn combat, take your turn immediately (once per round)
        Miss FortuneRe-roll 1d20 or 3 D/C per Luck spent; accept new result

        Personal trinket: once per quest outside combat, regain 1 Luck by reflecting on it.

        Combat

        Combat Sequence

        1. Initiator takes one turn before round 1.
        2. Order by Initiative (PER + AGI + modifiers), highest first.
        3. Each combatant takes one turn per round (1 minor + 1 major action).
        4. Repeat until conflict ends.

        Defense

        Attack difficulty = target's Defense (+ range, stealth, called shot modifiers).

        • AGI 1–8: Defense = 1
        • AGI 9+: Defense = 2

        Making an Attack

        1. Choose weapon and target (optional: called shot +1 difficulty).
        2. Roll attribute + skill; difficulty = Defense (+ range for ranged).
        3. On hit, roll hit location (1d20 table) unless location was called.
        4. Roll weapon D/C damage; subtract DR for damage type and location.
        5. Expend ammunition for ranged attacks as applicable.

        Attack Skills

        AttackTest
        Melee weaponSTR + Melee Weapons
        UnarmedSTR + Unarmed
        Small GunsAGI + Small Guns
        Big GunsEND + Big Guns
        Energy WeaponsPER + Energy Weapons
        ThrownAGI + Throwing or PER + Explosives
        HamonEND + Hamon (energy)

        Stand (Power)Power (numeric) + skill

        Range Modifiers

        Ranged and thrown attacks use the weapon's ideal range (Close, Medium, Long, or Extreme). Difficulty increases by +1 per range band away from ideal — whether the target is closer or farther.

        Weapon RangeTarget at CloseTarget at MediumTarget at LongTarget at Extreme
        Close (same zone)+0+1+2+3
        Medium (adjacent zone)+1+0+1+2
        Long (2 zones)+2+1+0+1
        Extreme (3+ zones)+3+2+1+0

        Add these modifiers to the attack difficulty (alongside Defense, cover, called shots, etc.).

        Reach: If an enemy is within Reach, non-melee ranged attacks and other non-melee tests against them gain +2 difficulty (stacks with the table above).

        A hunting rifle (Long range) at Medium distance: +1 difficulty. The same rifle at Close range: +2 difficulty. A combat shotgun (Close range) at Long distance: +2 difficulty.

        Damage Effects

        Weapon and stand attacks list damage effects (Burst, Vicious, etc.). When you roll damage D/C, each die showing an Effect (5–6) triggers every effect on that attack. Multiple Effects on one roll stack — two Effects on a Piercing 1 weapon ignore 2 DR. Full rules in Chapter 4 — Equipment and the appendices.

        Injuries & Dying

        Critical Hits & Injuries

        If a single attack deals 5 or more damage after DR to one hit location, that location suffers an injury. Injuries impose ongoing penalties until properly treated.

        LocationInjury Effect
        ArmCannot use that arm; drop held item
        LegFall prone; lose Sprint; Move costs a major action
        Torso2 D/C bleed at end of each of your turns
        HeadLose your next turn; vision-based tests +2 difficulty

        Penalties last until the injury is treated (First Aid) and then fully recovered (rest — see Chapter 5).

        Dying

        When a character is reduced to 0 HP, they immediately suffer an injury to the location just struck, fall prone, become unconscious, and begin dying.

        • If the attack that reached 0 HP was also a critical hit, the character suffers two injuries: one for the critical hit and one for hitting 0 HP (both on the location struck unless the GM rules otherwise).
        • While dying: no actions (minor or major). First Aid cannot restore HP — only stabilize (below).
        • At the start of each of the dying character's turns in initiative, their player rolls END + Survival. Difficulty = number of injuries currently suffered. Complication range 19–20. On a pass, they remain dying. On a fail, they die.
        • If a dying character suffers any further damage, they immediately gain one additional injury (in addition to any injury from a new critical hit).

        ◆ Stabilizing the Dying ◆

        A dying character can be pulled back from death by stabilizing — restoring them to 1 HP and ending the END + Survival death saves.

        MethodActionTest / Effect
        First AidMajor actionINT + Medicine, difficulty = patient's injuries (+1 if treating yourself). On success, choose one: stabilize (1 HP, no longer dying), heal HP equal to your Medicine rank, or treat one injury
        Medic perkFirst AidStabilize a dying patient at difficulty 0
        Rokakaka FruitMinor actionStabilize at 1 HP, restore 4 HP, or treat one injury; then roll Equivalent Exchange (see equipment)

        After stabilization: the character remains unconscious until someone spends 1 AP to rouse them, or until they receive further healing. They are no longer dying and resume normal turn options once awake.

        Treated Injuries

        A successful First Aid roll can treat an injury so its penalties no longer apply. The injury is not fully healed — only patched.

        If the character later takes damage to a location with a treated injury, roll 1 D/C after applying damage. On an Effect (5–6), the wound reopens and the injury penalties return.

        Major Actions

        • Attack — melee or ranged
        • Defend — AGI + Athletics vs current Defense; success +1 Defense (+1 more for 2 AP)
        • First Aid — INT + Medicine
        • Stand Attack / Stand Ability — see below
        • Ready, Rally, Sprint, Assist, Pass — as core rules

        Minor Actions

        • Aim, Draw Item, Move (1 zone), Interact, Take Item (Rokakaka, etc.)
        • Summon Stand — first activation in fight
        • Hide Stand — stand inactive but ready

        Healing in Combat

        During a fight, only quick interventions are available. Full recovery happens outside combat (Chapter 5).

        • First Aid (major action): INT + Medicine — stabilize dying, heal HP (= Medicine rank), or treat one injury. See Injuries & Dying above for difficulties.
        • Rokakaka Fruit (minor action): 4 HP, treat 1 injury, or stabilize at 1 HP; then roll 1 D/C — Effect = Equivalent Exchange complication
        • Bandage (minor action): 1 HP; cannot stabilize or treat injuries
        • Blood Pack (minor action): vampire only, restore 3 HP

        Dying characters cannot regain HP from First Aid until stabilized. Allies may use the Assist action to add dice to Medicine tests.

        Environment

        Combat locations are divided into zones (rooms, street sections, rooftops, etc.). Range bands measure distance between attacker and target across those zones.

        Zones & Range Bands

        BandDistanceNotes
        ReachArm's lengthMelee range; +2 difficulty to non-melee tests vs targets in Reach
        CloseSame zoneDefault for most pistols and melee
        MediumAdjacent zoneOne zone away
        Long2 zonesSnipers, rifles
        Extreme3+ zonesLongest practical shots

        On a Move minor action you enter a zone and may position anywhere in it (including Reach of objects or enemies). Sprint reaches up to two zones away.

        Cover

        Cover is the primary way the environment reduces damage. Objects between attacker and target provide bonus DR against physical and energy attacks by rolling extra D/C dice — not a flat number, and not added to attack difficulty.

        1. Qualify for cover: The character must be behind an object that obscures the hit location about to be struck. A waist-high wall protects the torso but not the head on a called head shot. If cover blocks the attacker's line of sight entirely, the target cannot be attacked until they move or the cover is removed.
        2. Roll cover D/C: When a qualifying physical or energy attack hits, roll the cover's listed D/C dice before subtracting DR. Add the total to the target's DR for that attack only (armor DR + cover DR + type matching).
        3. Resolve damage: Subtract combined DR from damage as normal. Cover does not apply to poison, sunlight, or other types unless the GM rules the material blocks them (e.g. lead glass for sunlight — rare).
        4. Prone: While prone, a character behind cover may re-roll their cover D/C once and keep the better result.
        5. Breaking cover: The Breaking damage effect permanently reduces cover by 1 D/C die per Effect rolled (see Chapter 4). Reduced cover persists until repaired (INT + Repair, difficulty 2) or the object is destroyed.
        Cover TypeD/C DiceExamples
        Light1 D/CBushes, curtains, folding screen, thin wooden door, café table
        Standard2 D/CThick wood, rubble pile, sedan door, brick low wall, market stall
        Heavy3 D/CConcrete pillar, marble statue, steel shutter, bank counter
        Fortified4 D/CVault door (ajar), sandbag emplacement, reinforced blast wall
        Example: Jotaro ducks behind a marble pillar (3 D/C) as DIO shoots. The hit lands on the torso for 4 damage. Jotaro has 1 physical DR from his coat. Cover rolls 2 + 1 + 0 = 3. Total DR = 1 + 3 = 4. He takes 0 damage; the pillar's cover is still intact unless DIO's attack had Breaking.
        Stands & cover: A stand in a separate zone does not grant cover to its user unless the stand physically interposes (GM discretion). Remote stands attacking from behind environmental cover use the same cover rules for attacks against the stand if it can be targeted.

        Difficult Terrain & Obstacles

        Moving out of difficult terrain or across an obstacle between zones costs extra AP in addition to the movement action. If you lack AP, use Rally (STR + Athletics, difficulty 0) to generate AP first.

        Terrain / ObstacleExtra AP
        Thick mud, loose sand, stairs+1
        Swamp, unstable rubble, waist-high barrier+2
        Steep slope, fast water, chest-high wall or long jump+3

        Light Step perk ignores difficult terrain AP costs.

        Environmental Conditions

        Conditions may affect whole scenes or specific zones.

        ConditionEffect

        Fog / rain+1 difficulty on ranged attacks at Medium+ range; heavy rain +1 to Energy Weapons

        Hazards

        Hazards inflict damage when a creature enters or starts its turn in the zone. Typical saves: AGI + Athletics or END + Survival, difficulty set by the hazard.

        HazardDamage
        Open flame2 D/C energy
        Raging fire3 D/C Persistent energy
        Falling (per zone)3 D/C Stun physical
        Live wire / discharge3 D/C Stun energy
        Chemical spill2 D/C poison
        Collapsing debris (trap)3–6 D/C physical; AGI + Athletics diff 2 to halve

        Dangerous objects (mines, tripwires, pressure plates) trigger on entry or on a complication; PER + Survival or AGI + Athletics to avoid before damage resolves.

        ◆ Chapter Four ◆

        Equipment

        Money

        Track wealth as a single abstract total. Rarity 0–5 sets base cost. Wealth rating for NPCs uses the same dice as money loot.

        Weapon Qualities

        Fire Rate (FR): Ranged attacks may fire up to FR times per major action; each shot after the first adds +1 difficulty unless a perk says otherwise. Each shot expends ammunition.

        Range: Close (C), Medium (M), Long (L), Extreme (X). Use the Range Modifiers table in Chapter 2 for attack difficulty.

        Two-Handed: Requires two hands; cannot use a shield or second weapon.

        Inaccurate: Cannot benefit from Aim; called shots cost +2 difficulty instead of +1.

        Blast: Hits all targets in a zone; each defends separately. Cover applies.

        Throwing: Usable at thrown range with AGI + Throwing; uses listed damage and effects.

        Damage Effects

        Listed on weapons, explosives, stand attacks, and hazards. Trigger only when one or more Effects appear on the damage D/C roll (each die showing 5–6). Count the total number of Effects rolled — that number applies to every effect on the attack.

        EffectRule
        Burst For each Effect rolled, the attack hits one additional target within Close range of the primary target. Resolve each hit separately (same difficulty as the original attack). Each extra target costs 1 additional ammunition from the weapon. Burst cannot hit the same target twice.
        Breaking For each Effect rolled, permanently reduce the target's cover by 1 D/C of damage reduction. If the target has no cover, instead permanently reduce the DR of the location struck by 1 — physical damage reduces physical DR only, energy reduces energy DR, sunlight reduces sunlight DR, poison reduces poison DR. Armor DR can be restored with INT + Repair (difficulty 2) and appropriate parts.
        Persistent If one or more Effects are rolled, the target suffers the weapon's full damage again (re-roll D/C, apply DR) at the end of their next turn and at the end of each subsequent turn for a number of rounds equal to the Effects rolled. A variant type may be noted in brackets — e.g. Persistent Energy, Persistent Sunlight, Persistent Poison — in which case the recurring damage uses that type instead of the weapon's base type. The target may spend a major action to attempt to end Persistent damage early: END + Survival (or INT + Medicine for poison), difficulty = number of Effects originally rolled.
        Piercing X For each Effect rolled, ignore X points of DR on the hit location, where X is the rating (Piercing 1, Piercing 2, etc.). Apply after determining location; only affects DR matching the attack's damage type unless the profile states otherwise. Hamon and sunlight damage always ignore DR against vampires regardless of Piercing.
        Spread For each Effect rolled, the attack inflicts one additional hit on the same target. Each extra hit deals half the rolled damage (round down) and strikes a random location (roll hit location), even if the initial attack was a called shot. DR applies separately to each hit.
        Stun If one or more Effects are rolled, the target cannot take their normal minor and major actions on their next turn. They may still spend AP to buy extra dice or take bonus actions granted by perks or stand abilities. Standing up from prone if knocked down still costs actions as normal once the stun ends.
        Vicious For each Effect rolled, the attack inflicts +1 damage after the D/C roll is totaled and before DR is subtracted. Vicious stacks with critical injuries and other bonuses.
        Sunlight Replaces Radioactive from the base 2d20 rules. For each Effect rolled, the target suffers 1 additional sunlight damage after normal damage from the attack is applied. Sunlight is an energy subtype: it ignores vampire DR, uses the Sunlight DR track on the character sheet, and is not reduced by normal energy DR unless the profile says otherwise. Against non-vampires, sunlight damage counts as energy damage. Hamon exposure uses energy/sunlight rules against vampires (×2 damage where noted). Track sunlight damage separately when an attack mixes types (e.g. 4 physical + 1 sunlight from one roll).
        Example: A Tommy Gun (4 D/C, Burst) hits with 2 Effects on the damage roll. The primary target takes full damage. Two additional targets within Close range are also hit; each takes a fresh 4 D/C roll, and 2 extra rounds of ammunition are spent.
        Stacking: An attack can have multiple effects (e.g. 4 D/C Energy Persistent Sunlight). Roll damage once; each Effect triggers all listed qualities. A Plasma Rifle with Persistent and 2 Effects both recurs for 2 rounds and benefits from any other listed effects on the same roll.

        Gamemastering

        GM Responsibilities

        • Arbitrate rules, set difficulties, run NPCs and stand abilities
        • Manage AP pools (yours and group's)
        • Adjudicate stand visibility, Equivalent Exchange, sunlight
        • Award XP for milestones and defeated threats

        Difficulty Guidelines

        Routine 0–1, challenging 2–3, heroic 4–5. Stand battles: +0–1 for visibility, range, or unusual abilities.

        NPC Types

        TypeSPECIAL TotalSkillsHP
        Normal35 + ⌈lvl/2⌉2 tags @2; INT+lvl pointsEND + lvl
        Notable42 + ⌈lvl/2⌉3 tags @2; INT+lvlEND + lvl + LCK
        Major49 + ⌈lvl/2⌉4 tags @2; INT+lvlEND + lvl + 2×LCK

        Creatures (Non-Stand)

        Use BODY + MIND = 8 + ⌈lvl/2⌉. Melee/Guns/Other skills. HP = BODY + lvl. Defense usually 1. Examples: vampire thralls, beasts, zombies.

        Stand NPCs

        Character block (S.P.E.C.I.A.L.) + stand block (Power/Speed/Range/Durability/Precision/Development Potential). GM runs both on one initiative. PC and NPC stand grade budgets use 41 + ⌈level/2⌉ as the sum of numeric grade values. Iconic villains in Chapter 7 may exceed this; Tool-type stands may be lower.

        Leveling NPCs

        • +1 SPECIAL odd levels; +1 skill; +1 HP
        • Every 2 levels: +1 D/C on one attack or upgrade gear
        • Stand: may improve one grade or add ability when DP allows

        Grouped NPCs

        2–5 identical NPCs: one leader rolls, others assist; +1 D/C damage per extra member.

        XP Awards

        Listed per example NPC. Defeat or overcome challenge. Milestone leveling optional.

        Complex Stands (GM-Built)

        Funny Valentine (D4C): parallel zones as separate scenes; swap requires 2 AP and touch.
        Wonder of U: environmental calamity hazard — AGI+Athletics to avoid, not a combat profile.
        Tusk ACTs: Development Potential unlocks ACT 1–4; nail shots use Precision.

        Safety

        Session zero: lines, veils, stand violence tone. X-card or equivalent encouraged.
